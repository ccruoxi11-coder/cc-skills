# PRISMA 2020 — systematic reviews & meta-analyses

**Source:** Page MJ, McKenzie JE, Bossuyt PM, et al. *The PRISMA 2020 statement: an updated guideline
for reporting systematic reviews.* BMJ 2021;372:n71. A **27-item** checklist + the **PRISMA 2020 flow
diagram** (and PRISMA-S for search reporting, PRISMA for Abstracts). Verify against the official files.

> Use for any structured literature synthesis (e.g. "systematic review of deep learning for X").
> Register the protocol (PROSPERO) before screening.

## Checklist domains and high-miss items

**Title / Abstract**
- Identify the report as a systematic review; structured abstract.

**Introduction**
- Rationale; **objectives with an explicit question** (PICO / PIRD).

**Methods**
- **Eligibility criteria**; **information sources** (each database + dates); **full search strategy**
  for at least one database (PRISMA-S); **selection process** (independent reviewers, automation);
  data-collection process; data items; **risk-of-bias assessment** method (e.g. QUADAS-2/PROBAST);
  effect measures; **synthesis methods** (pooling model, heterogeneity I², subgroup/sensitivity);
  reporting-bias assessment; **certainty assessment** (e.g. GRADE).

**Results**
- **Study selection with the PRISMA flow diagram** (records identified → screened → excluded with
  reasons → included); study characteristics; risk of bias of included studies; results of individual
  studies; **syntheses** (forest plot, heterogeneity); reporting biases (funnel/Egger); certainty.

**Discussion**
- Interpretation; limitations of evidence and of the review process; implications.

**Other**
- **Registration & protocol** (PROSPERO id); support/funding; competing interests; **availability of
  data, code, and other materials**.

## Cross-disciplinary red flags (AI systematic reviews)

- No registered protocol; single-reviewer screening; no reproducible search string.
- Missing PRISMA flow diagram; no risk-of-bias tool applied to included AI studies.
- Pooling clinically/technically heterogeneous models; ignoring publication bias.
- Not extracting AI-specific fields (modality, validation type, reporting-standard adherence).
