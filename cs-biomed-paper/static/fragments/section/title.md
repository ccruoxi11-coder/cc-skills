# Section: Title

## Rules

- Name the **problem + approach + (where possible) the clinical/biological object**, not just the
  method. "X-Net" alone is weak at biomedical venues.
- Be honest about scope: don't imply a population or generalization the data don't support.
- Avoid hype words (`novel`, `powerful`, `revolutionary`) and undefined acronyms.
- Consider stating the study type if the venue expects it (e.g. "…: a multicenter retrospective study").

## Patterns

- `[Method] for [clinical task] in [population/modality]: [study type]`
- `[Biological finding] revealed by [computational approach]`

## Pitfalls

Over-broad claim in the title that the abstract then has to walk back; method-only title with no
clinical hook; acronym soup.

Draft 3–5 options; pick with the user. Mark `[待补充：研究类型 / 目标人群]` if unknown.
