# Section: Related work

## Rules

- Organize by **theme / approach family**, not one-paragraph-per-paper.
- Cover **both literatures**: clinical methods for the task *and* the CS/ML methods — reviewers from
  each field check their own.
- For each thread, end with what it does *not* solve → this is your gap (consistent with stage 02).
- Be fair: position your work as an extension/combination, not by dismissing prior work (see ethics).
- Distinguish what you *read and verified* from what you cite second-hand.

## Pitfalls

- "Annotated bibliography" with no synthesis or comparison.
- Missing the other field's literature entirely.
- Straw-manning prior methods to inflate novelty.
- Citing benchmarks/datasets without their original papers.

In some venues this merges into the Introduction — confirm with the user / venue fragment.
