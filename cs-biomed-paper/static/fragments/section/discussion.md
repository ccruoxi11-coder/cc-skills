# Section: Discussion

## Default structure

`principal finding (in clinical/biological terms) → comparison with prior work → why it works /
mechanism → clinical or biological implication → limitations → future work`.

## Rules

- Open by restating the **principal finding and what it means** for a decision or for understanding —
  not a results recap.
- Compare honestly with prior work, including where others did better.
- **Name the limitations the experiments revealed** — sample size, single-modality, retrospective
  design, external-validation scope, failure modes. Reviewers trust papers that state their own
  limits; hiding a known one invites rejection.
- Keep claims bounded; separate *demonstrated* from *plausible* (`suggests` / `may`).
- Translate CS gains into clinical/biological significance — a Δ in AUC must be linked to impact.

## Pitfalls

- Restating Results without interpretation.
- Over-generalizing beyond the studied population/modality.
- A token "limitations" sentence that omits the real weaknesses (leakage risk, no external cohort,
  class imbalance).
- Claiming clinical readiness without prospective evidence.
