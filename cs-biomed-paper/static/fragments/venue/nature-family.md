# Venue: Nature family (Nature subjournals, npj, Nat Med, Nat BME, Nat Comms)

## Framing

- **Lead with significance for a broad readership.** The opening must make a non-specialist see why
  this matters before any method detail. Nature-family abstracts/intros funnel from importance → gap →
  what was done → result → implication.
- **Claims must be exceptionally well-supported** — external validation and clinical/biological
  significance are effectively required, not optional. Over-claiming gets desk-rejected fast.
- Structured for impact: a strong summary paragraph; methods often at the end or in supplement.

## Mechanics

- Strict data/code availability and reporting-standard compliance; reporting-summary forms are common.
- Tight main-text figure/word limits; heavy use of supplementary/extended data.
- Cover letter must state the advance and why it fits *this* journal's scope.

## Pitfalls

Method-first framing with no broad significance; incremental delta dressed as a breakthrough; weak
data-sharing; ignoring the reporting summary. Confirm the exact subjournal scope: `[待补充：目标子刊]`.
